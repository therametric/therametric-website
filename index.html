Dr. George K. Stookey, Ph.D.

Former President & CEO · Therametric Technologies, Inc.

🕊 In Memoriam · 1934 – 2022

A native of Indiana, Dr. Stookey received his bachelor's degree in chemistry from IU in 1957, followed by a master's in preventive dentistry (1962) and a doctorate in dental sciences (1971). He joined IU's faculty in 1964 and was promoted to Distinguished Professor in 1998.

Dr. Stookey's primary research interests included fluoride pharmacology, the prevention of dental caries, early caries detection technologies, and calculus prevention. He received the International Association for Dental Research Distinguished Scientist Award in 2002.

Though Dr. Stookey passed away in January 2022, his legacy of scientific excellence lives on through Therametric Technologies and the researchers he inspired.

IRB stands for Institutional Review Board — a group of individuals formally created under federal law to review and approve clinical research studies involving human subjects. The IRB's primary responsibility is to protect the safety and rights of study participants by ensuring:
  • Risks to participants are minimized
  • Risks are reasonable in relation to potential benefits
  • The Informed Consent Document accurately describes all procedures, risks, and benefits
  • All study activities are conducted ethically
Most clinical research studies cannot begin without IRB approval.
